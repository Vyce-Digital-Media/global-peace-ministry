3. ABOUT US PAGE

Page Title
About Global Peace Ministry & Our Founder

About the Ministry
Global Peace Ministry is a Christian, interdenominational, non-governmental organisation (NGO) dedicated to proclaiming the Kingdom of God, promoting world peace, healing the sick, delivering the oppressed, and helping the less privileged across all nations.
Founded and led by Lord Professor Dr. Kingsley Kebiru Momodu, the ministry operates from its base in Romford, Essex, London, United Kingdom, with an active international outreach across Africa, Europe, the Americas, and Asia.
We believe that peace begins in the human heart, is nurtured in community, and can — through God's grace and collective human will — transform nations. Our work is not merely spiritual: it is practical, compassionate, and rooted in a conviction that every human being deserves dignity, freedom, and hope.

His Story
Born in Nigeria into a family that straddled both Islamic and Christian traditions, Dr. Momodu's spiritual journey — from Islam to Christianity — did not breed contempt for either faith, but deepened his reverence for the common spiritual hunger that unites all human beings. This interior journey, accomplished not in bitterness but in understanding, gave him a perspective that few scholars of peace can claim: the lived experience of inhabiting two of the world's great traditions of compassion.
He has travelled extensively across Africa, Europe, the Americas, and Asia — sitting in the palaces of the powerful and in the dwellings of the destitute. He has spoken in universities and in prisons, in cathedrals and in community halls. He has broken bread with kings and with the homeless. Wherever he has gone, he has carried the same message: that every human being is worthy of dignity.
Based in Romford, Essex, London — one of the world's most culturally diverse cities — he continues to lecture, write, and advocate for the realisation of global peace.

4. OUR MINISTRY & SERVICES PAGE

Six-Fold Ministry of Global Peace Ministry

1. Global Peace Advocacy & Prayers for the World
At the heart of everything we do is a burning conviction: that global peace is possible. We pray for the world daily — for nations in conflict, for governments and leaders, for refugees and displaced peoples, and for the ordinary men, women, and children who bear the weight of war, injustice, and division.
We organise and participate in international prayer events, peace conferences, and awareness campaigns. Through the academic and theological work of our founder, we contribute to the global conversation on what it takes to build lasting peace.
"Pray without ceasing. In everything give thanks." — 1 Thessalonians 5:17

2. Charity Work & Foundation — Helping the Less Privileged
We believe that faith without works is dead. Our charity arm reaches out to the most vulnerable members of our communities — the homeless, the hungry, the sick, children in need, widows, and orphans — providing practical support, food relief, financial assistance, and emotional care.
If you wish to support our charitable work or partner with us to reach more people in need, please visit our Contact page or Donate section.

📷 IMAGE PLACEMENT: Photo of ministry gathering/event with group of people

3. Preaching, Healing Prayer & the Prophetic
We are a ministry of the Word and the Spirit. Our meetings include powerful, Spirit-filled preaching from the Scriptures, prayer for physical and emotional healing, and prophetic ministry — where God speaks specifically into the lives of individuals, families, and situations.
Our meetings are interdenominational, welcoming people of all Christian backgrounds. They typically last between 2 to 5 hours, depending on the need of the gathering. You can attend our meetings or request one-on-one ministry simply as you are — God meets you where you are.
"The Spirit of the Lord is upon Me, because He has anointed Me to preach good news to the poor. He has sent Me to proclaim release to the captives... to set free those who are oppressed." — Luke 4:18

4. Bible Studies, Christian Living & Discipleship
We run regular Bible study sessions designed to help believers grow in their knowledge of the Scriptures and their personal relationship with God. Topics include foundational Christian doctrines, practical Christian living, understanding the gifts of the Spirit, prayer and fasting, the character of Christ, and how to navigate life's challenges with Biblical wisdom.
Our discipleship approach is warm, accessible, and suited to all levels — from new believers to mature Christians seeking to deepen their walk with God.

5. Global Evangelism & Help Ministry
The Great Commission calls us to go into all the world. Dr. Momodu has preached and ministered across the United Kingdom, Nigeria, Italy, and many other nations. Our ministry is committed to taking the Good News of Jesus Christ to every people group, every culture, and every corner of the earth.
We also operate a Help Ministry — a practical arm of our evangelism that meets the immediate needs of people we encounter on the mission field, recognising that people are more ready to receive the Gospel when their physical needs are first acknowledged.

📷 IMAGE PLACEMENT: Photo of Dr. Momodu at RCCG Praise Sanctuary Parish, Padova, Italy — group with congregation

6. Knowing Your Calling — Living God's Will for Your Life
One of the most powerful gifts we offer is personal ministry to help individuals discover, understand, and step into their God-given calling. Many people live unfulfilled lives because they have not understood why God created them or what they are specifically called to do in this world.
Through one-on-one counselling, prophetic ministry, and Biblical teaching, we help individuals understand their unique purpose, identify their spiritual gifts, and take practical steps towards living the life God designed for them.
If you would like to book a personal session, please get in touch via our Contact page.

About This Book
This landmark work explores the profound yet often overlooked truth that peace is not merely a distant aspiration, but an inherent human capacity. Drawing on a life shaped by migration, intercultural encounters, and rigorous academic research, the author examines how individuals and societies navigate diversity across religion, language, and culture.
From childhood experiences in Nigeria where Islam and Christianity intersected — to years of living in the multicultural landscape of London — the narrative reveals how everyday acts of kindness and coexistence form the foundation of global harmony.
Blending personal testimony, scholarly analysis, and insights from interviews and surveys conducted across multiple nations, the work investigates the conditions that nurture peace and those that suppress it. Through stories of forgiveness, reconciliation, and shared humanity, the book argues that global peace is achievable when institutions, communities, and individuals consciously cultivate empathy, dialogue, and mutual respect.
Ultimately, World Peace presents peace not as an abstract ideal but as a practical, collective responsibility for humanity.

"Global Peace Is Possible"

Research Foundation
The doctoral research that underpins this volume was conducted at Kennedy University of Baptist and employed rigorous academic methods: quantitative survey data from 300 participants across multiple countries, 25 in-depth qualitative interviews, and detailed case studies of successful peace processes from South Africa to Northern Ireland to Colombia. This empirical foundation supports the philosophical and theological arguments developed throughout the book.
